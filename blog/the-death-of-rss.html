The Death of RSS: Why Your Newsletter Needs a Modern Engine

For a long time, RSS was the backbone of the internet. But the internet has changed, and your newsletter workflow needs to change with it.

If you’ve ever tried to set up an automated newsletter, you’ve likely run into a brick wall known as the RSS Feed. You find a website you love, you want to share their articles with your audience, but when you look for that orange RSS icon... it’s nowhere to be found.

Why RSS is Fading Away

RSS feeds are becoming a thing of the past. In the early 2000s, every website built one by default. Today, modern web builders and social-driven platforms often ignore them entirely. Many creators want to keep you on their site, so they don’t make it easy for "bots" to pull their content.

This leaves newsletter curators in a tough spot: If there’s no RSS feed, do you have to copy and paste everything manually?

"The biggest hurdle to newsletter automation used to be the technical hunt for a feed URL. We decided to delete that hurdle entirely."

Enter the Autolett Engine

What makes Autolett so powerful is that we don’t care if a website has an RSS feed or not. We’ve developed a one-of-a-kind process for pulling articles from literally any website.

Our custom scraping engine doesn't look for a hidden code file; it looks at the website just like a human does. It identifies the headlines, grabs the high-resolution images, and extracts the links—all from a simple URL.

Any Website. Any Article.

Because we aren't tethered to the "old way" of doing things, your sources are now infinite. Want to pull from a niche industry blog that hasn't updated its tech in a decade? It works. Want to pull from a brand-new, flashy landing page that uses the latest JavaScript? It works.

You simply grab the URL where the articles live, save it to your Sources tab, and Autolett turns that static page into a live, permanent feed for your newsletter.

Future-Proof Your Curation

Relying on RSS is like trying to play a Blu-ray on a VCR. It was a great standard once, but the world has moved on. By using Autolett’s URL-based sourcing, you are future-proofing your business. You’ll never have to worry about a source "breaking" just because they updated their website and forgot to fix their RSS link.
